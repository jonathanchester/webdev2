Johannes Itten was a Swiss Expressionist painter, designer, teacher, writer and theorist associated with the Bauhaus (Staatliches Bauhaus) School.

Togther with German-American painter Lyonel Feininger and German sculptor Gerhard Maracks, under the direction of German architect Walter Gropius, Itten was part of the core of the Weimar Bauhaus.

I. LIFE AND WORK

1904-1908

From 1904 to 1908 he trained as an elementary school teacher. Beginning in 1908 he taught using methods developed by the creator of the kindergarten concept, Friedrch Frobel, and was exposed to the ideas of psychoanalysis.

1909

In 1909 he enrolled at the Ecole des Beaux-Arts in Geneva but was unimpressed with the educators there, and returned to Bern.

Itten's studies at the Bern-Hofwil Teachers' Academy with Ernst Schnider proved seminal for his later work as a master at the Bauhaus.

Itten adopted principles espoused by Schnider, including the practice of not correcting his students' creative work on an individual basis, for fear that this would crush the creative impulse. rather, he selected the certain common mistaked to correct for the class as a whole.

1912

In 1912, he returned to Geneva, where he studied under Eugene Giliard, an abstract painter.

he was heavily influenced by Adolf Holzel and Franz Cizek.

Itten opened a private school in Vienna using the work and textbook of Eugene Giliard as a base. From Holzel, Itten adopted a series of basic shapes (the line, the plane, the curcle, the spiral) as a means from which to begin creation, and the use of gymnastic excercises to relax his students and prepare them for the experiences that where to occur in the class.
